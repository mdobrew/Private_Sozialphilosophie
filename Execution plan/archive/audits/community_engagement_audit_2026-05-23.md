# Community-engagement track audit — 2026-05-23

*Audit subagent report. Read-only review of the Phase II + Phase III community-engagement track currently distributed across `programme_parameters.md` §2.4 + §3.6 and `execution_plan_v1.md` Appendix G + inline M22+ entries. Triggered by user note that "this track was defined as the ultimate goal but never really second-guessed."*

## Headline verdict

**The track is structurally under-specified and back-loaded.** The activity *mix* is roughly right; the *sequencing* is wrong in three places; the *interlocutor list* is competent but narrow; and the *goal articulation* understates the gap to "recognised interlocutor." The current design treats community engagement as a thin layer on top of reading work. To actually serve the long-term goal, it needs to be (a) started 5–6 months earlier, (b) thicker by ~30–40h across Phases II–III, (c) augmented with an output channel other than the M44 piece, and (d) re-sequenced so the interlocutor map drives the *back half of Phase II*, not its closeout.

The current plan is sufficient to *submit a piece with two presentations and a couple of cold emails*. It is not sufficient to be a *known name* in the Frankfurt-tradition community by M44. The honest framing is: this is a minimum, not the target.

## Specific recommendations

### Add

1. **Earlier low-stakes presentation at the ECB (~M22, ~8–10h).** A 30-minute informal talk at an ECB internal seminar / brown-bag (research department, or one of the discussion circles in DG-Research / DG-Economics) presenting a *Skizze*-derived piece — e.g., the *Verdinglichung*-on-DSGE Anwendung or the value-freedom-of-projections piece. Audience is sympathetic but trained; gives a stage rehearsal in front of *real* critical questions long before M37. The current plan has the first oral presentation at M37–39 — fifteen months after Phase II opens. That is too long without a feedback event.
2. **One book review for *DZfPh* or *WestEnd* in Phase II (~M24–M26, ~20–25h including the read).** Phase II-appropriate target: a recent Stahl, Celikates, Vogelmann, or Jaeggi monograph. *DZfPh* and *WestEnd* publish reviews by non-academics; the lead time is short (3–4 months); it gives the user a *byline* in a field venue **before** the Phase III piece arrives in editors' inboxes. This is the single highest-leverage missing item. W3.3 (the M38 critical review) currently has no publication target attached — it is treated as a writing exercise. Make it a submission.
3. **Translation contribution as an opt-in (~M28–M32, ~15–25h).** If a Frankfurt-tradition figure (Stahl, Celikates, Allen) has a recent English piece without a German version, propose translation to *WestEnd* or *Mittelweg 36*. Very low intellectual risk, very high name-recognition return. Mark as [BONUS — opt-in].
4. **A standing reading-group / scholar-correspondence channel.** Identify a Frankfurt-tradition reading group with public participation (FAPTF runs one; the IfS-adjacent *Kritische Theorie Kolloquium* has been online since 2020). Joining one before M27 gives recurring low-cost contact, not just episodic event attendance. Budget ~1h/month from M22 onward (~15h total).
5. **3–5 substantive scholar emails over Phase II–III, not 1–2 at M42–M44.** See sequencing below.

  - Total: 3–4 emails, ~12–15h, vs. current 1–2 at ~8h. The increment is small; the visibility increment is large.

## On the interlocutor list

The current five (Herzog, Stahl, Beckert, Forst, Hartmann) are well-chosen *given the economist-of-the-ECB framing.* The list is biased toward bridge-figures, which is correct for the contribution's likely shape. But it under-weights the *Sozialphilosophie centre of gravity*. Concretely:

- **Add Rahel Jaeggi (HU Berlin).** Conspicuous absence. *Kritik von Lebensformen* and *Fortschritt und Regression* are likely Phase III-load-bearing texts; she runs the *Centre for Humanities and Social Change* and is the most cited living Sozialphilosophin under 65. If the contribution touches forms-of-life critique or progress-claims, she is the modal reviewer.
- **Add Robin Celikates (FU Berlin).** Active on critique-methodology and on the postcolonial challenges of Module 8A. Co-author of the *Einführung* the user reads at M1. He runs FAPTF — which the plan already targets for the informal presentation. **Direct relevance to the plan's own logistics.**
- **Add Daniel Loick (Amsterdam) or Eva von Redecker (Verona/Berlin).** Active frontier voices, both writing on capitalism / property / domination in ways adjacent to the likely Phase III terrain. Loick especially if the contribution drifts toward Marx-recovery.
- **Consider Frederik Vogelmann (Bremen).** If 9.6 *Die Wirksamkeit des Wissens* is read (currently Phase IV-deferred), he is a natural correspondence target — younger, accessible, active in critical-theory.net.
- **Consider Sergej Seitz (Vienna) or Mirjam Wenzel.** Younger-generation voices.

**Khurana, Allen (Amy), Menke, Honneth himself, Forst, Saar.** Khurana, Menke, Saar: probably too senior and stylistically distant for cold contact to land; treat as audience, not interlocutors. Allen: relevant especially for Module 8A; worth tracking but unlikely first-correspondence target. **Honneth himself: do not write. The cold-email-to-Honneth path is high downside (silent rejection or perfunctory reply, which closes the channel), low upside.** Forst: keep on list but the user's piece needs to be *defensibly Forstian* before contact, otherwise it reads as economist-trying-on-justification.

**Verdict on list:** Add Jaeggi and Celikates (these are the central omissions); add one of Loick/von Redecker; the rest is fine.

## Risk-flags

1. **Highest risk: the M44 submission goes to a journal whose editors and likely reviewers have never heard the user's name.** Three Vorlesung semesters and five event attendances do not produce name recognition. A book review, an ECB-internal talk write-up shared with the FAPTF circle, and 3 scholar emails over 14 months do. **This is the single most consequential audit finding.**
2. **Interlocutor map at M27 means the Phase III topic at R5 (M30) is selected without a clear sense of *who in the field can read it.*** The map needs to be in hand at R5, not generated at the same time. Move to M22.
3. **Single informal presentation at M37–39 is the first time the user defends an argument out loud in 30+ months of self-study.** That is too high-variance. An ECB-internal warm-up at M22–24 hedges this.
4. **W3.3 critical review is a sunk asset.** Writing 1,500 words of publishable review and *not submitting* it is a strict pareto-loss against submitting.
5. **No fallback if Goethe Vorlesungen don't align thematically.** Plan-B venues (HU Berlin online lectures, IfS public series, Forst's *Normative Orders* recordings) are not flagged.

## Gap to "recognised interlocutor"

Honestly stated: **the M44 endpoint is roughly 40–50% of the distance to "recognised interlocutor" in the Frankfurt-tradition Sozialphilosophie community.** What the current plan produces:

- A submitted (not necessarily published) piece
- Two presentations on the record
- 1–2 scholar contacts initiated post-submission
- Vorlesung Gasthörer-status across two semesters
- An interlocutor map in the user's notes

What "recognised interlocutor" actually requires, baseline:

- One published piece (peer-reviewed, not preprint) in a field venue
- A second piece in active drafting or under review
- 2–3 scholars who have read the user's work and can name him in conversation
- An institutional affiliation beyond "ECB economist" (Gastwissenschaftler, associate-researcher, IfS Förderkreis, FAPTF active member)
- Demonstrated participation in field debates (panel discussant, workshop participant beyond presenter, journal referee work — the last typically requires the first publication)

The M44 endpoint reaches roughly the first three at a minimum and none of items 4–5. **Phase IV is where items 4–5 are built**, conditional on M44 success. The bridge §2.4 framing is realistic *if and only if* M44 lands. The book-review-in-Phase-II recommendation buys insurance for the case where M44 doesn't.

**Reframing recommendation:** the user should stop calling M44 "community entry" and start calling it "the *first* visible move in community entry, which Phase IV completes." The current framing creates a false-closure expectation that the plan cannot meet.
