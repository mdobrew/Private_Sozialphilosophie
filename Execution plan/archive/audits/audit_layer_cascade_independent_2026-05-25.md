## 1. Explicit cut criteria (the three axes)

Each baseline-active item is scored along three axes. Layer placement follows from the score profile, not from "what v2.0 / v2.1 happened to defer."

### Axis 1 — Field-importance criticality (per y3 v2.1 §3)

- **Structural** = MUST-Anchor *or* an item on which downstream Anchors depend (e.g., 0.2 Honneth "Pathologien" anchors the diagnostic frame the rest of the programme operationalises; Heinrich 2.3 is the methodological scaffold for primary Marx and for any value-form Phase III piece). Cutting forces topic re-scope or breaks a chain.
- **Important** = MUST-Engage / SHOULD-Light items carrying non-trivial apparatus or load-bearing reading-order role (e.g., 6.7 *Strukturwandel*, presupposed by Fraser 7.12; 9.3 Celikates, presupposed by 9.4 Jaeggi-Ideologiekritik).
- **Optional** = COULD / SHOULD-Orient / [RS] secondary; partial-cuts cost coverage but not apparatus.

### Axis 2 — Interlocutor value (per bridge §2.4)

- **High** = bridge §2.4 names the author as a Phase II–III contact target *and* the work is on the syllabus. Live test: would cutting create a credibility hole the user must fill before a Q&A, cold-email exchange, or workshop discussion? High includes 9.6 Vogelmann, 8.10 Jütten, 14.8 von Redecker, 14.1 Streeck (Jens Beckert / MPIfG terrain), 8.6 Allen / 8.12 Jaeggi / 7.10 Forst (Tagung-modal reviewers).
- **Medium** = sometimes cited in current debates; community engagement would not directly demand it but absence is noticeable in M37–M42 reviewer norms. Examples: 7.13 Renault, 7.17 Bedorf, 9.7 Boltanski *OC*, 11A.1 Foucault *SuP*, 8.9 Rosa *Resonanz*.
- **Low** = primary-content load-bearing for the user's own apparatus but not interlocutor-exposed at Phase II–III (Hegel *RP*, Marx *Kapital* ch. 1 — these are Structural anyway, so axis-2 doesn't drive their layer placement).

### Axis 3 — Hour cost (from current Inv)

Per-item all-in hours from Inventory tables (post-2026-05-25 Khurana repair). Bigger items released earlier give more hour-yield per cut decision; small items are "free" only because the cost of *not* cutting them is also small.

## Bottom line

The independent reconstruction validates the existing §A.1's core structure (Layer 0 buffer, Layer 4 slip, Layer 5 M46 extension) but reorganises Layers 1–3 along the three-axis framework. **Three concrete recommendations**:

1. **Demote 7.13 Renault, 7.17 Bedorf, 9.7 Boltanski to Layer 2** (Medium-interlocutor); promote 7.14, 7.11, 11A.4, 14.9, 9.9 as Layer 1 (genuinely Low-interlocutor + low hours).
2. **Promote 14.8 von Redecker and 8.10 Jütten to Layer 3 with override-required language** — current Layer 2 placement is annotated as conflict-flagged by §A.1 itself; the framework should encode the §2.4 protection at the layer level, not as a user-decision footnote.
3. **Split Phase III conditional-MUST decisions into a distinct "Layer C"** evaluated at R5, separate from the falling-behind cascade. Layer C is topic-commitment-driven; Layers 0–5 are capacity-pressure-driven; conflating them weakens both.

Structural floor sits at ~1,218h, ~385h below v2.1 baseline ~1,603h. The cascade between baseline and floor is the user-decision surface — and that surface should be partitioned by the three axes (criticality / interlocutor / hours), not by historical-rebuild grouping.
