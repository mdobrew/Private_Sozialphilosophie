# Community-engagement audit — second opinion (2026-05-23)

*Independent second-opinion audit. Did not read first audit. Briefed with user's reframed goal: become an interdisciplinary outsider-insider participating in the Frankfurt-tradition conversation, NOT a Frankfurt-tradition PhD academic. Anti-hallucination guidance baked in (after the first audit's `critical-theory.net` invention).*

## 1. Headline verdict

The current track is **competently designed for the wrong goal**. It is calibrated for *academic-insider community entry*: attend the Vorlesung, sit through events, climb the informal→formal presentation ladder, then write substantive cold emails. Almost all the activities are *audience-side, low-signal, high-orientation*. The user is treated as a learner trying to be admitted.

For an outsider-insider profile, this is upside-down. The signal the user carries — *ECB economist who has actually done the Frankfurt-tradition reading and can engage on the terms of the field* — is **scarce, asymmetric, and underexploited by the current plan**. None of the proposed activities make that scarcity visible. Vorlesung attendance, public-event attendance, and 1–2 cold emails do not differentiate the user from a curious academic-adjacent person who reads.

The two presentations and the interlocutor map are the *only* high-signal items, and they're back-loaded to Phase III. The corrective is not to add hours — the 25–30h/year budget is right — but to **rebalance the mix toward activities where the user's profile is the value-add**. I'd reallocate roughly 30–40% of the hours.

## 3. Activities currently in the plan that are low-leverage

**(a) Vorlesung Gasthörer attendance (Phase II).** This is the largest single time commitment (~15h) for the lowest interpersonal-signal return. As orientation it might help, but the user reads philosophical German fluently and has the apparatus — the marginal pedagogical value over Module 6–7 reading is small. And Gasthörer status is *invisible*: you don't talk, you don't get noticed, you don't compound any relationship. I'd **downgrade Vorlesung to "attend selectively when topic aligns; skip if not"** and reallocate ~10h to activity (a) or (c) above. Keep an *Oberseminar* slot in Phase III — that involves discussion and is high-signal — but the Vorlesung is largely content-extraction that the syllabus already covers.

**(b) "3–5 public events at IfS / ConTrust / Goethe" as listening-only.** Same diagnosis. Listening-only event attendance is low-signal even if useful. **The fix is small but important: every event attended should target one *post-event* interaction** — a question in Q&A, a 5-minute coffee-break exchange, a follow-up email of 4 sentences referencing something specific the speaker said. Without that, you've spent 3h to learn things you could have learned faster from the speaker's paper. Frame the events as *"places to start one conversation,"* not *"places to absorb."*

**(c) The "substantive cold emails to 1–2 scholars at M42–M44" item.** Cold emails to senior scholars at the end of Phase III are the *least* leveraged version of relationship-building. By that point the user has either already met these people (via activities a/b/d above) — in which case the email is a follow-up, not a cold approach — or hasn't, in which case the email lands as an unsolicited paper from an unknown sender at the worst possible moment (busy senior people, end of semester). I'd **drop the cold-email item entirely as a distinct task** and replace its hours with a *relationship maintenance* line item across Phase II/III: 1–2 substantive exchanges per quarter, growing organically from events, the ECB invitation, or the discussant role.

**(d) "Informal then formal" two-presentation ladder, *as such*.** The ladder itself is sound. But the user's profile means he doesn't need to climb the standard amateur-to-Tagung ladder — the *Tagung* itself, for him, is a reasonable second appearance. An ECB economist on a Frankfurt-tradition panel is *already* the unusual presence the room notices. The informal pre-presentation should be re-scoped from "low-stakes practice" to *"working draft feedback at a specific high-trust venue"* — ideally FAPTF, which is real and the right level, or a one-person feedback session with Herzog/Stahl/Hartmann. **The user should not "practise" being a speaker; he should optimise the informal venue for feedback quality, not stakes-gradation.**

## 6. Phase IV long-game (post-M44, 5+ years)

If the M44 piece lands and the engagement track has worked:

1. **Standing referee work.** Once published in *WestEnd* / *Constellations* / *DZfPh* / *Philosophy & Social Criticism*, the user becomes referee-eligible. Low-cost (~3–4 reviews/year × ~4h each) and *the single highest signal of insider status* that requires no further career investment. Sign up to ORCID + journal referee databases at M44.

2. **Recurring ECB-hosted format.** If the M22–M25 talk worked, propose it as an annual or biannual format. This makes the user a fixed node in the network: scholars know there's a "Frankfurt economist who hosts these things."

3. **Visiting fellow / associated researcher arrangements** — at IfS, ConTrust, or MPIfG, on a part-time / non-residential basis. Realistic at the 2–3-years-post-publication mark, conditional on a second piece in progress.

4. **One co-authored piece on the methodological intersection.** With Herzog, Jütten, or Vogelmann — *not* a Frankfurt-tradition piece per se, but a "what quantitative method can/can't say about social-form claims" piece. Natural sequel to the M44 article; opens venues unreachable from either side alone (*European Journal of Social Theory*, *Journal of Economic Methodology*).

5. **Co-organised workshop.** Once 2–3 relationships are stable, co-organising a small Frankfurt-or-Cologne workshop on (e.g.) "Normative Reconstruction and Macroeconomic Method" is realistic. Highest-status / lowest-effort-per-status-unit community-engagement act, because organisers control guest list and agenda.

6. **Keep writing one short essay per year for *Soziopolis* / *Geschichte der Gegenwart* / *Mittelweg 36*.** Low cost, high visibility, identity-maintaining. This is what sustains the "in the conversation" status over years.
