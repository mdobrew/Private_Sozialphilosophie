# Cut-list review — Phase II Sozialphilosophie programme

*Subagent report, 2026-05-22. Triggered by ground-up Hrs recomputation showing Phase II inventory inflation from ~504h to ~791h all-in. Reviews the existing 5-layer cut schema and proposes deeper cuts.*

## Headline diagnosis

The cut-layer structure was calibrated against the *old* ~504h Phase II inventory. At ~791h all-in, the existing layers (1–5) together yield ~70–90h of trimming and a ~1-month calendar slip — which still leaves the user roughly 5–6 months short of M44. The structure also confuses two distinct levers (item-defer, tier-downgrade, calendar-slip, scope-thin) that need to be addressed separately. The cuts most worth keeping are those that protect the W3.2 piece against reviewer challenge on social-pathologies, ideology critique, and capitalism-as-form. The cuts to deepen are scope concessions inside the big Honneth and Jaeggi monographs — which are inflating because of the "one Recon per chapter" convention, not because the texts are too big.

## 4. Layer 4 review

The current Layer 4 framing — "M30 → M31 calendar slip = ~20h decompression" — is incoherent at the new inventory. It buys 1 month of calendar against an 8-month gap and does not actually relieve hours, only redistributes peak load.

**Proposed Layer 4 reformulation:**

> **Layer 4 (structural calendar-extension).** Accept Phase II calendar extension by N months at fixed ~8 h/week weekly budget. N is determined by current Phase II inventory residual after Layers 2–3 cuts: N = ⌈(residual_hrs − 8×weeks_remaining) / 32⌉. Cascade: Phase III start slips by N months, submission target slips by N months. **This is the lever for accepting the new inventory totals without scope concessions.** Suggested ceiling: N = 4 (submission M44 → M48). Beyond N = 4, scope concessions (Layer 6+) are preferable.

At ~791h inventory − ~34h Layer 2 − ~12h Layer 3 = ~745h, against ~12 months × ~36h/month ≈ ~432h of budgeted Phase II hours: residual ~313h gap. At ~8h/week marginal, that's ~39 additional weeks ≈ **9 months**. Layer 4 alone cannot close this honestly; combined with Layers 5–6 it can.

## 5. Layer 5 review

Current Layer 5 — "Accept full revised timeline extension to M46 (W13 calibration cascade response)" — is now an under-specification. M46 implies only +2 months of calendar relative to M44 baseline; at the new inventory this requires substantial scope concessions, not just a calendar extension. Two problems: (a) Layer 5 doesn't name the concessions; (b) the M46 target is no longer realistically defensible at the new inventory totals.

**Proposed Layer 5 rewording:**

> **Layer 5 (calibration cascade + scope concession bundle).** If W13 calibration trigger fires with capacity at steady-state, *and* Layers 2–4 are exhausted: activate the structural scope concessions in Layer 6 below (per-chapter-Recon convention relaxation + Honneth 7.1 thematic Recons + Jaeggi 8.2 selective chapters). Target M46–M48 submission, depending on how many Layer 6 concessions are accepted. M46 requires the per-chapter-Recon convention relaxation as a minimum; M48 is the floor at which all Layer 6 concessions plus Layer 4 calendar slip combine.

The honest re-target is **M46–M48 range, contingent on scope concessions**, not "M46 as default fallback."

## Verdict (R4 pre-emptive activation recommendation)

At R4 (M16), the user should pre-emptively activate **L2′ Rosa→Light + L3′ Jaeggi-Fraser 8.5→Light + L6a per-chapter-Recon-convention-relaxation (Honneth 7.1: 9→4 Recons, Jaeggi 8.2: 10→6 Recons)** — together ~62h of cuts at near-zero W3.2 defensibility cost — *and simultaneously commit to the M48 submission target rather than M44*. M44 is no longer honestly reachable at the new inventory totals without scope concessions that *do* compromise W3.2; M48 with the L6a per-chapter-convention relaxation as a built-in baseline parameter is defensible, leaves headroom to *rescue* Boltanski to Light tier (closing the lay-normativity reviewer-exposure gap), and preserves the option of conditional Aglietta-rescue at R5 if the W3.2 topic commits to capitalism-as-form. The reviewer-defensibility-protecting cuts (Rosa-Light, 8.5-Light, per-chapter→thematic) all *improve* W3.2's compositional readiness; the temptation to defer Boltanski for hours-savings should be resisted. M46 should be treated as a stretch target, not a default.
