## 4. Trade-offs identified

**(a) Premature commitment risk.** The conditional set is the sharpest case. Pre-blocking ~55h for "2-of-4 fires" anchors the topic decision toward the conditional set as the dominant Phase III reading commitment — risks the M30 R5 candidate-topic outline being shaped by what's pre-blocked rather than by where the user's professional asymmetry actually bites. **Mitigation**: keep P3.Inv.4 as a single generic row labelled "2-of-4 fires" without naming which two; let R5 fill it.

**(b) Bottom-up rigour vs flexibility at M37–M39.** Pre-blocking too sparsely (e.g., ~30h reading at M37–39) would force the Schedule's "Targeted frontier reading ~35–40h" row to be ungrounded — month-row hours would lack Inv backing. The heuristic builds in ~47h at M37–39 (P3.Inv.4 + P3.Inv.5 + P3.Inv.6 share), which gives Schedule rigour while leaving R5 room to redistribute within the bucket.

**(c) Targeted research bucket sizing.** This is the load-bearing trade-off. The Phase III topic isn't chosen until M30 R5 with lock-in at M34 — most "targeted research" is post-lock-in (M34–M39). I sized P3.Inv.6 at ~30h because v2.1 baseline absorbed the heavy SHOULDs in Phase II, so Phase III doesn't need to re-do that frontier work. If the M34 lock-in surfaces a topic that requires substantial Appendix C / Module 14 pull-forward (e.g., capitalism-as-form route ⇒ Brenner + Backhaus), this bucket needs to grow to ~50–60h — which would consume the 50h Header delta from §3.

**(d) Writing-block leave hours are reading-blind.** W1 (M36) and W2 (M43) are *writing* weeks, not reading. The heuristic correctly puts reading hours *low* at M36 (~3h) and M43 (~3h) to reserve leave for drafting/revising. This matches the bridge §2.4 norm.
