## 4. Feasibility judgement per class

**Dense DE: 5 → 6 pp/h (+20%).** The hardest call. This is Hegel *Rechtsphilosophie*, Marx *Kapital*, Adorno *DdA* + *Minima Moralia* + *Negative Dialektik*, Lukács *Verdinglichung*, Horkheimer 1937, Mannheim, Husserl. The published rates I've seen cited (Adler/Doren on philosophical reading; the working assumption in German philosophy MA programmes) cluster at **3–6 pp/h for genuinely dense German philosophy on first encounter, rising to 6–8 pp/h on second encounter when the apparatus is in place**. 5 pp/h is at the optimistic end of first-encounter; 6 pp/h presumes you've already crossed the threshold. Reading practices v4 §C.2 already documents this — "Dense DE Mid 3.5, Slow 2.5" implies the bottom-of-range 5 pp/h is *already* the fluent-reader/minimal-re-read figure. For *Rechtsphilosophie* §§142–256 specifically, going to 6 pp/h means ~5h instead of ~6h on the 30pp Civil Society block — feasible *only if* you don't actually stop to track the Hegelian moves (Neuhouser § references, the gear-shift between abstract right and *Sittlichkeit*, the move into *Pöbel*). At 6 pp/h on a first read you will decode the words; you will not retain the argument structure for the Practice A reconstruction. **Verdict: not realistic at first encounter. Honest at second encounter (re-read phase, Phase III Comparison Exercise).**

**Systematic DE: 8 → 9 pp/h (+12.5%).** Habermas *TKH* + *EuI* + *F&G*, Forst *RauR* + *N&P*, Honneth monographs (under decouple), Khurana, Gadamer, Postone. The Honneth-decouple rule already protects the bigger writing cost (you write at Layered α, not Systematic α); the reading speed is what's at stake. Habermas's TKH is dense-but-clearly-structured — once you grasp the architecture of system/Lebenswelt or the three knowledge-constitutive interests, pages move. **9 pp/h is realistic for the second half of a chapter once the apparatus is laid; not for the first half.** Forst *RauR* is more uniformly dense (the right-to-justification machinery doesn't have the same "architecture comes into view" moment). **Verdict: realistic for Habermas-style architectonic prose; aggressive for Forst monographs and *EuI*. Marginally OK; the +1h on a 12h chapter is within the natural variance of "good week vs bad week."**

**Layered DE: 10 → 11 pp/h (+10%).** Jaeggi *Entfremdung* + *KvL* + *F&R*, Celikates KsP, Stahl IK, Rosa *Resonanz* + *Beschleunigung*, Bedorf, Heinrich (intro text). For a German-fluent reader with prior Frankfurt exposure, **11 pp/h on Jaeggi is honest** — her prose is genuinely clear, the conceptual layering is in the structure not in the sentence. Same for Stahl, Celikates, Rosa. The 10% bump is within the slop. **Verdict: realistic across this class.**

**Analytic EN: 18 → 19 pp/h (+5.6%).** Fricker, Hollis, Woodward, Mead, Forst articles, Fraser articles. Within natural variance; not a meaningful change. **Verdict: trivially realistic.**

**Transparent EN: 20 → 21 pp/h (+5%) / DE: 18 → 19 pp/h (+5.6%).** Neuhouser, McCarthy, Outhwaite, Streeck, Polanyi, Wiggershaus, Pippin, Heinrich, Freyenhagen. Already paced at the fluent-reader rate for what are deliberately accessible texts. **Verdict: trivially realistic.**

**Where +1 pp/h compromises comprehension quality.** The threshold lives where the reading rate forces you to *stop tracking the directed-against structure* (Practice A component iii). For systematic texts (Habermas, Forst monographs, Honneth *KuA*) the bind is at the *Verbindungsstellen* — the joints between sub-arguments — which is where the apparatus actually does its work. At 9 pp/h on Habermas TKH ch. I, you will track validity claims as a list; you will not retain the move from validity claims to communicative rationality as a *grounding* relation, which is the point. For Dense DE, the threshold is more brutal: at 6 pp/h on *Kapital* ch. 1 §4 (commodity fetishism), you will read the famous "table dance" passage and miss why Marx wrote a paragraph of phenomenology in the middle of a logical argument — and that's the bit you'd cite in W3.2.

**Differential by text type.** +1 pp/h is *much more feasible on secondary monographs* (Neuhouser, Heinrich, Wiggershaus, Pippin, Freyenhagen, McCarthy, Outhwaite) than on the primary dense texts (Hegel RP, Marx *Kapital*, Adorno DdA + ND, Lukács, Horkheimer 1937). On the secondary literature, +1 is honest. On the primaries, +1 specifically degrades the Practice A reconstruction quality you've explicitly designed the programme around. Articles (Forst noumenal-power, Fraser NLR, Zurn, Laitinen-Särkelä) are already paced at the variance threshold — +1 pp/h is invisible.

## 5. Recommendation

**Do not adopt +1 pp/h across the board.** The current bottom-of-range is honest as a *planning estimate* — and `reading_practices_v4.md` §C.2 explicitly frames it as the fluent-reader-with-minimal-re-read figure. Inflating it ~10% on average to absorb capacity overage is exactly the kind of move that produces the "calibration trigger fires at W13" outcome the plan already anticipates.

**Adopt selectively, only for Transparent and Analytic.** +1 pp/h on Transparent and Analytic classes saves ~9h across the active inventory; the increment is within natural variance and the text-type is structured to be read at fluent speed. This is honest. **Leave Dense, Systematic, and Layered DE alone.**

Even more pointedly: **what +1 pp/h on Dense / Systematic DE actually represents is the post-W13 calibration position, not the pre-calibration baseline.** If the W13 trigger evaluation shows actual measured pp/h on the first Hegel chapter (1.2) and the first Habermas reconstruction (5.2, 6.1) running at the +1 rate *with the Practice A reconstruction quality intact*, then the rate is real and the bottom-of-range should be re-anchored. Until that empirical check fires, the conservative figure is the disciplined one. The hour-budget overage (+26% at M44) is better closed by the §A.1 cut-list cascade (which makes scope cuts explicit) than by silently inflating reading rates (which makes the cut invisible). **The Layer 1 cascade saves ~62h with named texts deferred; +1 pp/h saves ~108h by absorbing the cuts into nominal speed-up. The former is honest about the trade-off.**

One operational alternative worth flagging: the **programme_parameters.md §8 fixed/revisable distinction** suggests the calibration anchors are revisable. A defensible move would be to set the Transparent/Analytic classes at +1 (Transparent DE → 19, EN → 21; Analytic DE → 16, EN → 19) in the matrix now, document the change, and leave Dense/Systematic/Layered DE at bottom-of-range pending W13 measurement. Savings: ~9h. Honest.
