## 1. Pacing realism — **Score: 2/5**

**Evidence.**

- **The default reading speed is the fastest band, not a conservative one.** "Calibrated to the bottom of the bridge's pacing range" (plan, header) sounds cautious, but the Style × Tier matrix defines bottom-of-range as *the fast end of each band* (plan Appendix A: Dense-class German at 5 pages/hour, versus a mid estimate of 3.5 and a slow estimate of 2.5). The entire ~1,506-hour demand tally assumes the reader hits the *best* case on every text for 42 months. Formula spot-checks confirm the Inventory faithfully implements the matrix (Neuhouser ~19.5h ✓, *Kapital* volume-1 selections ~16h ✓, Lukács ~26h ✓, *Kampf um Anerkennung* ~44h ✓) — the arithmetic is clean; the input assumption is the optimism.
- **Phase II is scheduled in the overage band as a 19-month baseline.** Plan (~line 1200): "~720h … At ~38h/month average over 19 months — within sustainable ~7.5–9 h/week rhythm." But the bridge document §2.1 defines ~8–9 hours/week as "sustainable for **short stretches** (e.g., the Module 7 peak)". 38 hours/month ≈ 8.8 hours/week *average* for 19 months, with the months-30-to-32 cluster at 38/42/40 hours/month — i.e., the plan's baseline sits permanently in what its own spec calls the voluntary-overage band. Phase-wise capacity check: Phase II capacity ≈ ~565h versus ~720h demand — **~+27% phase-local overage**, hidden inside the headline "+8.8%" programme figure because Phase I nets to ~0 and Phase III is under-envelope.
- **The ~40-productive-weeks-per-year premise is not reflected in the week grid.** Bridge Class A (fixed parameter): "~40 productive weeks/year (the other ~12 absorb annual leave, illness, family, ECB peaks)". Phase I schedules content into essentially all 65 calendar weeks (weeks 1–57 plus light buffers in weeks 58–65), averaging 486h/65 weeks = 7.5 hours/week *including* the ~15 weeks the spec says will produce zero. If those weeks actually produce zero, the remaining 50 need ~9.7 hours/week — above the sustainable band. Only ~6 buffer/light weeks exist, versus ~15 expected dead weeks.
- **Phase I has no shock absorber:** the plan itself notes Phase I has "effectively no first cuts available beyond Layer 0 bonus … remaining headroom ~2–5h".
- **Community-engagement hours are under-budgeted.** The Phase II total carries "community ~30h", but the plan's own listed Phase II engagement items sum to ~70–90h (book review 20–25h, cross-disciplinary short essay 10–20h, discussant slot 8–10h, interlocutor map 8–10h, Vorlesung ~15h, events ~10–12h, reading group). Bridge §2.4 itself budgets Phase II engagement at "~40–55h". The missing ~40–60h lands exactly on the months-27-to-33 reading peak. *(Auditor's sum — re-check the item list before acting on the exact figure.)*
- Mitigations that partially redeem the score: the week-13 two-gate calibration trigger with a *pre-committed* month-50 response; the Governing-Council-week reroutes; the Part 0 ramp. These handle reading-speed risk well; they do nothing about the Phase II structural overage, which is a scheduling decision, not a pacing measurement.

**Fix (within revisable parameters — capacity and the month-48 target are Class A / fixed).** Make the Layer 3 non-conflict demotions baseline rather than reactive: execute the *Theorie des kommunikativen Handelns* volume-2 selections (6.5), McCarthy commentary chapters, Rosa *Resonanz* (8.9), Zurn's second-order-disorders article (8.3), Laitinen & Särkelä (8.4), the Jaeggi–Fraser debate volume (8.5), and Dewey (4A.1) at the Light tier that phase3_critical_path §8.2 already decided for them — the plan currently tier-bumps all of these to Full (see §8 below). That recovers ~40–55h inside Phase II without touching any MUST-Anchor. Simultaneously, restate the Phase II community budget honestly (~70h) and offset by pushing the cross-disciplinary short essay window to months 33–36. Target: Phase II ≤ ~660h ≈ 8.0 hours/week — inside the sustainable band with peaks, rather than a 19-month occupation of the overage band.

## 2. Retention & spaced review — **Score: 3/5**

**Evidence.**

- Genuinely good machinery exists: the three-generation Honneth "Pathologien des Sozialen" re-read (week 5 → retreat R3 → end of Phase III); the quarterly-milestone chain with forced comparison against prior quarterly milestones; retreat syntheses at R2/R3; the Mead re-read immediately before *Kampf um Anerkennung*; the pre-tagging of Phase II reconstructions for the months-37–38 Comparison Exercise; Practice F targeted re-reads (Hegel's *Rechtsphilosophie* §§142–180 at Module 7 close, Dewey at Module 8 close); the Craft-Block dissection re-reads; the month-32 Phase II review; the month-46 re-skim.
- **The gap is Phase I primary material across the months-16-to-35 desert.** Marx: *Kapital* chapter 1 §4 (the fetishism section) is last touched at retreat R2 (month 8); no scheduled re-entry before Phase III, and even the capitalism-as-form Layer C option (Aglietta) doesn't refresh Marx. Weber, Lukács, *Erkenntnis und Interesse*: no re-read between Phase I and the month-46 re-skim (which covers only Modules 9–10). If the publishable piece commits to the capitalism-as-form branch — one of the two starred keeper branches — the load-bearing Marx apparatus will be ~30 months cold at drafting time.
- Concept-level retention (Practice B concept entries in the Obsidian vault) is fed but never *reviewed* on any cadence. Nothing in 42 months schedules re-reading old concept entries or a rotating sample of the ~222-reconstruction corpus.
- The dangling ***Rechtsphilosophie* §§257–360 "Phase III re-read"** (Module 1 section: "Deferred: … → Phase III re-read") appears nowhere in the Phase III schedule or its heuristic inventory. It is deferred to a slot that does not exist — both a retention and a consistency defect.

**Fix.** (a) Add a standing corpus-review line to the module-close checklist (plan §D.2): 30–45 minutes re-reading three older Full reconstructions from *prior phases*, rotating by module — ~10h across the programme, and the only mechanism that touches Phase I material during months 16–34. (b) Insert the *Rechtsphilosophie* §§257–360 re-read explicitly into the Phase III topic-conditional research bucket with an hours line (~8–10h), conditional on a Hegelian/recognition topic — otherwise strike the deferral promise from Module 1. (c) Add one Marx re-entry item (~4h: *Kapital* ch. 1 §4 + a Heinrich chapter re-read) to the capitalism-as-form branch, firing with Aglietta.

## 3. Contingency & robustness — **Score: 4/5**

**Evidence.**

- This is the plan's strongest dimension. Triggers are measurable ("behind 2 / 4 / 8 weeks"), backed by the reading log's metrics; responses are graduated and pre-priced (Layer 2 ~65h of medium-interlocutor cuts → Layer 3 ~40h/~37h → Layer 4 = one-month structural slip to month 49 → Layer 5 = the full month-50 recalibration cascade). The 8-week rule ("Do not catch up. Re-enter at the next phase boundary") is the correct anti-collapse design. Phase III has its own event-driven slip protocol (conference call-for-papers rejected, feedback delayed, leave denied, draft overrun — each with a named response and the governing rule "slip the submission date, not the cycle"). The week-13 gate has a pre-committed cascade with second-order effects worked out. A 3-week illness is cleanly absorbed; a lost writing block has a named fallback month.
- Residual weaknesses keeping it from 5: (i) Phase I slack is ~2–5h, so every Phase I contingency escalates immediately to a 1–2-month structural slip — the 2-week trigger row ("drop bonus readings") is nearly vacuous in Phase I where bonus is already spent; (ii) **no protocol for a denied or lost transition retreat** (R1–R5) analogous to the writing-block rule — R5 in particular is a single point of failure (the Layer C firing decision + topic candidates); (iii) the "behind N weeks" triggers presuppose the week-grid realism problem in §1 — if every year predictably contains ~12 zero weeks, the 4-week trigger fires as a matter of arithmetic, not of trouble.

**Fix (minor).** Add one row to the Phase III slip protocol: "Retreat leave denied → convert to two consecutive weekends (~2×8h) within ±3 weeks of the boundary; R5's Layer C decision may not slip past month 36." And re-express the falling-behind triggers in *hours logged versus hours planned* (the log already records this) rather than weeks-behind, so predictable dead weeks don't masquerade as slippage.

## 4. Milestone measurability & feedback — **Score: 3/5**

**Evidence.**

- **Measurability is excellent.** Every module has countable outputs (reconstruction counts per text, comprehension checks against pre-stated capability targets, Dialogue Moment, adversarial notes, debate map), and the quarterly-milestone series measures *growth* against the reader's own prior attempts. Real go/no-go points exist with pre-committed responses: the week-13 gate, the week-38 *Negative Dialektik* decision, R5's Layer C firing, the month-38 topic lock-in, the Comparison essay as an explicit proof-of-positioning gate before the publishable piece.
- **External feedback is the problem: it arrives at ~month 22 at the earliest, realistically ~months 27–29.** Phase I is heads-down by design; until the reading group / cold emails (month 22+) and the book review (~months 27–29), the *only* check on whether two years of reconstructions systematically misread the tradition is Claude — and the bridge itself concedes the limits ("Dialogue Moments are pressure tests, not seminars") plus the hallucination caveat. A systematic misreading of Hegel or Marx formed in months 3–8 would be corrected, at best, 18 months later. The first feedback on *writing* in the field's genre (a book-review editor) lands at ~month 29 — six months before Phase III opens.
- The heads-down rule is bridge design content but is **not** a Class A fixed parameter (§6's Class A lists the engagement track's existence and hours, not its Phase I prohibition), so fixing this is legitimate.

**Fix.** Insert one external calibration event in late Phase I at near-zero cost to the heads-down rule: at the Motivational Checkpoint (weeks 55–57), send the Field Report's immanent-critique section as a one-page substantive question to one younger-generation scholar from the bridge's own list (Vogelmann, Jütten, von Redecker) — the bridge's cold-email norms already permit exactly this class of email from month 22; pull it forward once (~3h). Second, make the reading-group join (~month 22) a scheduled deliverable with a named fallback (an online colloquium) rather than "identify a reading group"; it is currently the only recurring external check before month 35 and has no failure mode written down.

## 5. Dependency ordering — **Score: 4/5**

**Evidence.**

- The macro-sequence is right and carefully engineered: Hegel → Marx → Weber → Lukács → first Frankfurt School → pragmatism → Positivismusstreit → Habermas → Honneth → Jaeggi, with deliberate scaffold-first moves throughout (Feenberg before Lukács; McCarthy before *Erkenntnis und Interesse*; commentary before *Theorie des kommunikativen Handelns*; Freud before *Dialektik der Aufklärung*; the Mead re-read immediately before *Kampf um Anerkennung*; Polanyi forward-shifted so Streeck is consolidation rather than fresh ground; Foucault at month 20 so his challenge is heard before Honneth's positive answer; the 2026-07-12 Kant→Khurana pre-block).
- But the plan violates **its own dependency annotations** (phase3_critical_path §4) in four places:
  1. "*Faktizität und Geltung* (6.6) is presupposed by Forst 7.7–7.10" — yet *Faktizität und Geltung* is *not in the baseline* (conditional only), while Forst's *Das Recht auf Rechtfertigung* (7.7) is a baseline MUST-Anchor at months 23–24. A MUST-Anchor is scheduled without its declared prerequisite.
  2. "Rosa *Resonanz* (8.9) ← Jaeggi *Kritik von Lebensformen* (8.2) + Rosa *Beschleunigung* (15.5)" — but *Beschleunigung* is never scheduled before month 28; its Part III appears only at the Craft Block (months 35–37), *after* *Resonanz*.
  3. "Freyenhagen (4.6) ← *Negative Dialektik* (4.5)" — the *Negative Dialektik* selections are Phase IV-deferred while Freyenhagen is core at weeks 38–40. Defensible (Freyenhagen is the *substitute* for *Negative Dialektik*), but then the dependency annotation is wrong, not waived.
  4. The *Rechtsphilosophie* §§257–360 re-read deferred "to Phase III" that Phase III never schedules (see §2).

**Fix.** One reconciliation pass on phase3_critical_path §4 against the executed schedule: either (a) annotate the *Faktizität und Geltung* prerequisite as "waived at baseline — *Erkenntnis und Interesse* + *Theorie des kommunikativen Handelns* vol. 1 carry the Habermas backdrop for Forst 7.7; 6.6 fires with the power/justification Layer C route", and mark the *Beschleunigung* / *Negative Dialektik* dependencies as satisfied-by-substitute; or (b) where the dependency is real (*Faktizität und Geltung* → Forst is, if the justification track is live at R5), add a conditional pre-read. Do not leave MUST-chain claims that the schedule contradicts — the dependency table is what R5-you will consult.

## 6. Phase III output realism — **Score: 4/5**

**Evidence.**

- The ramp is more credible than most self-study plans because writing starts long before Phase III: four Anwendungen (concept-applications) from week 24; the Field Report at week 57 (explicitly the primary seed of the publishable piece); five Skizzen from month 25; the book review and cross-disciplinary essay as *submitted* pieces; then a graded ladder inside Phase III (method note 1,000 words → writing exercise W3.1 at 2,000 words → Comparison essay 3,000 words as an explicit gate → the publishable piece W3.2 at 7,000–10,000 words). "Publishable" is operationalised via phase3_critical_path §1's reviewer norms — unusually concrete. Venue strategy, call-for-papers lead times, the two-presentation feedback structure, suggested-reviewer overhead, and the slip protocol are all in place. Hours: ~65h to first draft + ~30–40h substantive revision + ~30h in Writing Block W2 + ~20h final ≈ ~150–185h of writing on top of ~15 prior short pieces — tight but plausible for this profile.
- Two real weaknesses: (i) **Layer C frontier reading fires at months 41–43, *after* the months-38–41 first draft.** If Honneth's *Der arbeitende Souverän* (7.19) or Jaeggi's *Fortschritt und Regression* (8.12) turns out to preempt or reframe the piece's central claim, the discovery comes post-draft, with only the months-43–46 revision window to absorb it. The plan treats frontier reading as revision input; for the two 2023 monographs on the modal topics, it is *topic-selection* input. (ii) **No advisor-grade reader.** The "2–3 trusted readers" at month 41 are hoped-for products of the community track; if Phase II relationship-building underdelivers, the informal-presentation fallback presumes exactly the relationships that failed to form.
- Also: the writing blocks assume ~30h in exactly two leave weeks; the slip protocol covers denial but nothing covers a *low-quality* block (the block happened, the draft didn't converge) — the month-50 slip rule implicitly does.

**Fix (targeted).** Move a 10–15h Orientation-level pass over the two modal Layer C monographs (7.19 and 8.12: Einleitung + Schluss of each) from months 41–43 to months 37–38, before topic lock-in — funded from the Phase III targeted-research bucket, so no new hours. The Full/Light treatment still fires post-R5 as designed; the lock-in decision stops being made blind to the two books most likely to collide with it.

## 7. Motivation & sustainability — **Score: 3/5**

**Evidence.**

- Well-defended in design: the Anwendung series repeatedly re-anchors the programme in the reader's ECB identity (commodity form → financial product at week 24; *Verdinglichung* → DSGE models at week 31; instrumental reason → performance indicators at week 40); quarterly-milestone comparisons make growth *visible*; the Motivational Checkpoint is purpose-built and replaces reading rather than adding to it; the month-48 anchor is explicitly framed as psychologically load-bearing with sustainability trumping it on conflict; economist's notes run throughout.
- Predictable abandonment points, and how the plan fares: (i) **weeks 47–57 (months 12–14)** — the ~78h Module 5 (Gadamer + *Erkenntnis und Interesse*, the driest stretch) lands exactly at the classic year-1 slump; the plan parries with the Motivational Checkpoint immediately after — good. (ii) **months 20–30** — ~320h of Honneth/Jaeggi systematic monographs with per-chapter Full reconstructions and no positive-voice output until the first Skizze at month 25; nine months mid-programme where the only products are reconstructions of other people. (iii) **months 27–33 — the worst:** the 38/42/40 hours/month reading cluster *plus* book review *plus* short essay *plus* interlocutor-map refresh *plus* opportunistic discussant slot, largely unbudgeted (§1). This is the highest-probability collapse zone in the whole programme and it sits immediately before Phase III — a collapse here forfeits the production phase.
- Long-horizon motivation beyond month 25 leans heavily on community engagement materialising. If it doesn't, months 25–35 are a 10-month tunnel.

**Fix.** Same lever as §1: de-stack months 27–33 by executing the phase3_critical_path §8.2 Light-tier decisions (frees ~40–55h precisely in Modules 8–10) and shifting the short essay to months 33–36. Additionally, move the **first Skizze earlier, to ~month 22** (post-*Kampf um Anerkennung*, pre-Forst): the Skizze cycle's stated prerequisite is a competing-positions landscape, and by month 22 the reader has Honneth vs. Fraser vs. Foucault — enough for a first positive claim. That converts the months-20–30 reconstruction tunnel into one with a mid-point authored output.
