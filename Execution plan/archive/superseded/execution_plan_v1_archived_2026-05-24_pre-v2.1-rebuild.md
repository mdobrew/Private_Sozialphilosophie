If capacity is still suppressed by lingering private commitments, defer the response to W17–W18 and re-measure pacing in W14–W17; the protocol activates only if midpoint pacing persists *once capacity has returned*. Slow pacing under low hours is not strong evidence; slow pacing under steady-state hours is decisive.

**R1 capacity check (W8).** Review hours-per-week actually invested W1–W7. If any 2 of the 4 most-recent weeks fell below 4h *and* private commitments are expected to persist past W12, extend the ramp by 2 weeks (W9–W10 become continued Part 0 / Neuhouser intro at reduced pace) and defer the W13 measurement window to W15. Otherwise proceed as planned.

## A. Falling-behind protocols

| Trigger | Action |
|---------|--------|
| **Behind 2 weeks** | Drop the current and next 2 weeks of bonus readings. Switch any Full-tier reconstruction in the current module to Light tier. |
| **Behind 4 weeks** | Skip to the next module-boundary. Treat skipped material as deferred; write one paragraph naming what was skipped and what it was supposed to deliver. Flag for return at the relevant Phase IV connection. |
| **Behind 8+ weeks** | Do not catch up. Re-enter at the next phase boundary. The retained material is the apparatus; the missed weeks are not. |
| **Seasonal expected slowdowns** | Light weeks pre-flagged in the schedule: ECB December lull, ECB Governing Council weeks, end-of-Q4 work surges. Light week = no Full-tier practice, ~3–4h of accessible reading. |

Cut order: (1) bonus readings → (2) Full-tier practice downgraded to Light → (3) Light-tier practice downgraded to Orientation → (4) defer texts to next module boundary → (5) reduce event attendance. ## B. Comprehension infrastructure

- **Text-level checks** (texts >10h reading) appear inline at the end of each text.
- **Module-level checks** reproduced from the syllabus appear at module end.
- **Quarterly milestones** anchored to module boundaries, not strict 13-week cadence. Format: read 1 recent article from *WestEnd* / *DZfPh* / *Constellations* (suggested article in the syllabus's Part IV §1–5 if no recent issue lands), note (a) what you can follow, (b) what you can't, (c) what specific concepts/thinkers you're missing. ~2–3h. Replaces that week's bonus reading.
- **Dialogue moments (DM)** follow each module-level check. Format: state the strongest objection to the argument you just reconstructed and try to answer it. Either in conversation with Claude (paste your reconstruction, ask: where is this misreading the standard scholarly view? what assumption is undefended?), with a colleague, or in writing.

### B.0 Reading Log

A single running document — the *Reading log* — is the spine of the comprehension infrastructure. Started in W1 and maintained throughout the programme. Suggested location: an Obsidian note (e.g. `Reading log.md` at the Sozialphilosophie vault root). The log has two parallel tracks:

**Track 1 — Marginalia.** Dated entries capturing what you noticed while reading: passages that resisted you, claims you doubted, terms whose use shifted, cross-references back to earlier modules. Not a full-blown reading note — short, fragmentary, dated. The function: at retreats (R1–R5), you re-read recent log entries to recover threads you wouldn't otherwise remember a month later. R2 and R3 in particular draw heavily on the log to write the synthetic overview pieces.

**Track 2 — Metrics.** Weekly numerical entries:
- *Hours actually invested* in core reading + practice (separately, if convenient).
- *Pages per hour* (pp/h) on the active dense and systematic texts, recorded per session or week.
- *Full-tier overhead multiplier* (every Full reconstruction week): total all-in hours ÷ nominal reading hours. Compare against the Section A target of ~1.55×.

The W9–W13 **calibration log** is a focused subset of Track 2 used to evaluate the two-gate trigger at W13: systematic pp/h on Neuhouser, dense pp/h on RP Preface + §§1–33, and the Full-tier overhead multiplier from the W5 Honneth reconstruction. The W13 evaluation result (trigger fires or doesn't) is written into the log either way — "no trigger" is also a finding.

Beyond the calibration window, the log's metrics function is to keep you honest about pacing drift (the §A falling-behind triggers all depend on it) and to surface module-level patterns that the at-a-glance tables won't show — e.g., systematic slowness on Adorno that doesn't appear on Hegel.

### Why this module matters

Lukács transforms Marx's analysis of commodity fetishism and Weber's diagnosis of rationalisation into a *total* diagnosis of bourgeois consciousness: the reification (*Verdinglichung*) of social relations under capitalism is not merely an economic phenomenon but pervades the entire structure of modern thought. This is the argument Horkheimer and Adorno radicalise in *Dialektik der Aufklärung*. Without Lukács, the transition from Marx and Weber to the first Frankfurt School has a missing step, and the concept of *Verdinglichung* — as it recurs in Honneth (2005), Berger & Luckmann, and Jaeggi's critique of Honneth — lacks its proper genealogy.

The skill: *holding two compatible-seeming frameworks far enough apart that you can locate the genuine point of disagreement, then judging whether the disagreement is resolvable, and if so on whose terms.*

**Phase A — Materials assembly (~3h).**
Pull together: (a) Module 7 Full-tier reconstruction notes on recognition spheres + normative reconstruction; (b) Module 7 reconstruction on Honneth *RdF* methodological introduction; (c) Module 8 Full-tier notes on Jaeggi problem-solving model + Allen's critique; (d) Zurn 8.3 reconstruction; (e) Laitinen & Särkelä 8.4 reconstruction; (f) Method note from W1 (Month 27–30).

**Phase B — Reading-pass on flagged passages (~4–6h).**
Re-read targeted passages (already tagged `→ M32–34` during Phase II):
- Honneth *KuA* chs. 5–6 (Missachtung → struggle for recognition as the engine of moral development).
- Honneth *RdF* methodological introduction (normative reconstruction).
- Jaeggi *KvL* ch. 3 ("forms of life can fail") + chs. 8–10 (problem-solving model).
- Zurn (second-order disorders).
- Allen *End of Progress* ch. 6 (the critique of the problem-solving model's implicit progress story).

**Phase C — Comparison essay (~6–8h, ~3,000 words).**
Structure:
1. **Reconstruct each thinker's account of social pathology** in 600 words each. Be precise about: (i) what makes something pathological; (ii) what's required for it to be diagnosed; (iii) what the theorist's normative apparatus needs to do the work.
2. **Identify the agreements.** Both: pathology is institutional, not just personal; both reject pure functionalism; both reject pure moralism; both are committed to immanence (the standard for critique is internal to social practices).
3. **Identify the disagreements at three levels:**
   - *Conceptual:* recognition vs. problem-processing as the diagnostic primitive. What does each register that the other misses?
   - *Methodological:* normative reconstruction vs. quasi-pragmatist immanent critique. Where do they read the *same* practice differently?
   - *Philosophy-of-history:* does either require a developmental story? Allen's challenge applies asymmetrically.
4. **Take a position.** Where is the disagreement resolvable, and on whose terms? Where is it irreducible? What does the irreducibility tell you about the field's open questions?
5. **Locate yourself.** Where does the ECB / monetary-policy / financial-stability material from your Field Report bite differently on the two frameworks?

**Phase D — Filing (~1h).**
File the essay. **This essay is the proof-of-positioning that you need before W3.2.** It is also the first draft of part of W3.2's introduction.

**Quarterly Milestone 6 (M32):** Read 1 article each from *WestEnd* and *DZfPh*/*Constellations*. Note the shift since QM5.

## A. Style × Tier matrix and time formula

This is the canonical hour-budget computation for every text in the Inventory tables. Per-text Hrs cells are derived from this matrix; module totals are sums.

### Formula

```
Reading hours = Pages ÷ pp/h(class, language, bottom-of-range)
Writing hours = min(Reading × α(class, tier), n_chapters × per-Recon ceiling)
Total Hrs    = Reading + Writing
```

Reading speed (pp/h) and writing multiplier (α) depend on the text's **prose-difficulty class** (Dense / Systematic / Layered / Analytic / Transparent). Per-text class assignments live in [reading_speed_classes.md](reading_speed_classes.md). The class is **independent of the syllabus tag** — a [P] text can be Dense, Layered, or Transparent depending on its prose.

### Reading speeds (pp/h)

Bottom-of-range (fast end of band) is used as the default; mid/slow are for future re-calibration (e.g., if W13 trigger fires).

| Class | DE Bottom | DE Mid | DE Slow | EN Bottom | EN Mid | EN Slow |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Dense | 5 | 3.5 | 2.5 | 6 | 4.5 | 3.5 |
| Systematic | 8 | 5 | 4 | 10 | 7 | 5.5 |
| Layered | 10 | 7.5 | 6 | 13 | 9.5 | 7 |
| Analytic | 15 | 10.5 | 8.5 | 18 | 12.5 | 10.5 |
| Transparent | 18 | 13.5 | 11 | 20 | 16.5 | 13.5 |

### Writing multiplier α (writing hours per hour of reading)

| Class | Full α | Light α | Orientation | Bonus/Deferred |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Dense | 1.0 | 0.4 | flat 0.25h per text | 0 |
| Systematic | 0.5 | 0.2 | flat 0.25h per text | 0 |
| Layered | 0.4 | 0.15 | flat 0.25h per text | 0 |
| Analytic | 0.3 | 0.12 | flat 0.25h per text | 0 |
| Transparent | 0.3 | 0.12 | flat 0.25h per text | 0 |

### Per-Recon ceiling (cap)

- **Full ceiling**: 2.0h per Recon (one chapter or major section)
- **Light ceiling**: 0.75h per Recon

Cap binds when a text has few chapters relative to reading hours (e.g., Marx ÖpM — single essay over ~8h reading; without the cap, α=1.0 implies 8h writing; with cap, writing = 1 × 2h = 2h).

### Per-tier output format

| Tier | Per-chapter format | Word target |
|---|---|---|
| **Full** | Reconstruction note per chapter/major section: four-part structure (claim / support / directed-against / presuppositions) + forward-looking closing sentence + `## Empirical presuppositions` sub-heading. Re-read time absorbed in α. | 500–800w/chapter |
| **Light** | Reconstruction note per chapter/major section: two parts (claim + directed-against). Closing sentence optional; no empirical-presupp sub-heading required. | 150–250w/chapter |
| **Orientation** | No filed reconstruction note. 1–2 sentence reading-log entry per text (date, text, "main point I want to retain"). | n/a |
| **Bonus/Deferred** | Not scheduled in core. Opt-in only if capacity allows. | n/a |

### Special rules

**Honneth-monograph decouple.** Honneth monographs (*Kampf um Anerkennung*, *Recht der Freiheit*, *Anerkennung* (2018), *Der arbeitende Souverän*) read at Systematic pp/h but write at Layered α. Monographs are denser-paced than essays but write at Layered cost-per-chapter.

**Aphoristic flag.** Texts flagged aphoristic (*Minima Moralia* selected aphorisms, Benjamin "Theses on History") stay in Dense pp/h, but `n_chapters` = thematic clusters of ~5–8 aphorisms each, not per-aphorism.

### What α covers

## D. Comprehension infrastructure (detail)

### D.1 Reconstruction-week protocol

Every Full reconstruction week (W5, W14, W21, W36, W44, W48 in Phase I; weekly schedule in Phase II) follows this canonical protocol on top of the inline week instructions:

1. **Practice A reconstruction** (500–700 words): four-part structure — claim / support / directed-against / presuppositions — per [reading_practices_v4.md](reading_practices_v4.md).
2. **Closing sentence** (~1 min): one sentence in the form *"If I were making this argument, the move I would change is X"* — name a premise, method, example, or directed-against opponent. Do not defend yet. Compounds across the programme as a record of your own developing judgments.
3. **H2 component (ii) — Empirical presuppositions** (~10 min): in the reconstruction note, under a subheading `## Empirical presuppositions`, record any empirical claim the author's argument depends on. Practised from M1 onward; pre-loads the full H2 evaluations from M19 onward. (See `tpl-reconstruction.md` — the heading already exists.)
4. **C1 seminar questions** (~20–30 min): file 2–3 questions at the bottom of the reconstruction note — tensions / ambiguities *within the author's own framework*, not things you didn't understand. See Practice C1 in [reading_practices_v4.md](reading_practices_v4.md).
5. **K Use 1 cross-reference at first reconstruction (W5 only):** after your written reconstruction is filed, paste it to Claude with the Claude Use 1 (see reading_practices_v4.md inline callout at Practice A) prompt for a check-against-misreading pass. Hallucination caveat per K Use 4 applies — treat the answer as a hypothesis to verify.

### D.2 Module-close checklist

Every module close (W17, W25, W28, W32, W41, W46, W54 in Phase I; M19, M22, M25, M27, M29 in Phase II; corresponding Phase III boundaries) carries:

| Item | What | Time |
|---|---|---|
| **Comprehension check** | Brief written answers to capability targets listed at module start. | 2–3h |
| **DM (Dialogue Moment)** | State strongest objection to the central argument you just reconstructed; try to answer. With Claude / colleague / in writing. | ~1h |
| **✏ H1 light-discrimination note** | External objection you were tempted to make → translation to internal critique. 150–250 words. **Cadence taper:** every module M1–M6; alternating modules M7–M14 (Phase II); ad-hoc Phase III. | ~30–45 min |
| **C1 tally** | Confirm 2–3 seminar questions filed per major text in the module (already done per §D.1; tally check only). | n/a |
| **✏ Practice C2 — cross-thinker question** | One cross-thinker or translation question. Form 1: *"Does X's framework require what Y calls Z, even when X claims it does not?"* Form 2: *"If we rewrite X's claim in Y's vocabulary, what is lost?"* File alongside G1. Apprenticeship for the M32–M34 Comparison Exercise. | ~30 min |
| **🗺 Practice D — Debate map + negative-space note** | Visual map: 2 axes × thinkers covered in the module; place each thinker; mark response arrows. Then 200-word **negative-space note**: name one constructible position no thinker in the module occupies and classify as incoherent / unmotivated / historically suppressed. | ~1.5–2h |
| **📋 QM (Quartals-Meilenstein)** *(W17, W30, M19, M27, M37 only)* | Recent-article comparison — read 1 recent article from *WestEnd* / *DZfPh* / *Constellations*; note what you can follow, can't, and what concepts/thinkers you're missing. Replaces that week's bonus reading. | ~2–3h |

**Module-close gross load:** ~5–7h non-QM weeks, ~7–10h on QM weeks.
