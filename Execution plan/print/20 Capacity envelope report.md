## Accounting convention (decision 7)

programme_parameters §2.1 (Class A) records programme demand as "~1,506h → +8.8% vs. the ~1,384h M48 envelope — resolved." That figure reproduces only under v2's inventory-basis accounting (per-text hours + selected add-ons, with module closes, productive practices, and the honest community budget left out — and with v2 module headline-targets that understated their own inventory sums; audit §8 items 5–6). **v3's like-for-like inventory total is ~1,292.5h text-hours (post-repricing); its all-in study-side total is ~1,786.5h (+22%).** The machinery for this was always present (M50/M52 fallbacks, the cut cascade, the W13/W32 gates); what changes is that the number is now visible. **Resolution:** the honest all-in accounting stands as this report's convention; the phase-local balances above are the plan's own numbers (updated for the formula repricing). M50/M52 remain gate-triggered fallbacks, not a pre-adopted baseline; the programme_parameters §2.1 Class A row update is an upstream propagation item.

## Productive-weeks reconciliation (per the ~40-productive-weeks premise)

- **Phase I:** 65 scheduled weeks, of which 5 are retreat weeks → 60 weekly-rhythm weeks. The ~40-weeks/year premise expects only ~50 of these to produce. The grid designates ~5 absorbers (W1–W2 ramp at reduced load, W59 buffer share, W62, W65); the remaining ~5–7 expected dead weeks are **not** absorbed in-grid. Consequence, stated plainly: at bottom-of-range pacing Phase I completes on time only in a better-than-premise year; under premise-typical attrition it runs ~5–8 weeks long, which is exactly the W13/W32 → M50 cascade. The gates are the absorber; the grid does not pretend otherwise.
- **Phase II:** 82 calendar weeks; premise expects ~63 productive. Phase demand ~747.5h ⇒ ~11.9h per *productive* week (~9.1h per calendar week). This is the +33% phase-local overage made concrete — it cannot be absorbed by dead-week arithmetic; the ratified de-stack (decision 8) already nets it down, and the residual is the accounting finding above.
- **Phase III:** 61 calendar weeks; premise ~47 productive; demand ~484h ⇒ ~10.3h per productive week, concentrated in M41–43 and the two writing blocks. Event-driven slack (Appendix A.2) is the absorber.
