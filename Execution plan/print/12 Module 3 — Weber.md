### Why this module matters
Weber's diagnosis of modernity as a process of rationalisation culminating in the "iron cage" (*stahlhartes Gehäuse*) is the *problem statement* the entire Frankfurt School tries to answer. His methodological writings on *Verstehen*, ideal types, and value-freedom (*Wertfreiheit*) define the position Habermas and Honneth react against. You also need Weber to understand why Sozialphilosophie insists on normativity where Weberian social science demands value-neutrality — and as an economist, this is the module where your own methodological self-understanding is most directly on the table.

> [!note] Economist's note (running throughout Module 3)
> This is the module where your methodological self-understanding is most directly engaged. Weber's *Wertfreiheit* is the implicit epistemology of mainstream economics. The Frankfurt tradition will, in Module 5, argue that *Wertfreiheit* is itself a normatively committed position. Track which Weberian commitments you keep and which you let go.
