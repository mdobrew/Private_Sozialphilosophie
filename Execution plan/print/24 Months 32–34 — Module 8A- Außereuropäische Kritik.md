### Why this module matters

The Frankfurt tradition's project of *immanent critique* presupposes a "we" whose immanence is taken as the standpoint of evaluation. Module 8A poses three traditions challenging that presupposition at distinct levels of severity: postcolonial (Said, Chakrabarty, Spivak) operates within the European critical-theory horizon and criticises specific exclusions; decolonial (Quijano, Mignolo) is frontal — coloniality is constitutive of modernity, European critical theory cannot redeem itself by adding voices; Black radical (Robinson) contests at the structural level — racial capitalism is constitutive of capitalism's form, not derivative.

For your Phase III contribution: any piece that touches the Honneth–Fraser axis, the recognition framework, or Module 14 / Appendix D capitalism critique should engage these challenges. Fraser herself draws on Robinson in *Cannibal Capitalism* (Module 14 item 14.2, Phase III reading); reviewers at *DZfPh* / *WestEnd* / *Constellations* increasingly expect engagement with at least one of these traditions.
