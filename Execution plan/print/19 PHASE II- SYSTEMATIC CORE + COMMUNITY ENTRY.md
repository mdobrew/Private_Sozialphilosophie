### Skizze cycle — Phase II definition and cadence

Beginning at the end of Module 7 (Honneth, ~M25), Skizzen run at ~6–8 week intervals through Phase II until W3.2 first-draft work begins. Each Skizze is a 1,000–1,500 word argumentative essay defending *one* positive philosophical claim of your own, in the Frankfurt tradition's *Versuch* genre. ~4.5–6h per Skizze including Claude iteration.

**Why M25 start.** Defending a positive philosophical claim requires having a competing-positions landscape (Honneth vs. Jaeggi vs. Allen vs. Forst) to position yourself within. Pre-Honneth attempts produce wisps.

**Phase II Skizze targets (~5 attempts, ~25h):**
1. **Skizze #1 (~M25, post-Honneth):** Recognition and one specific phenomenon — e.g., "Recognition theory needs an account of institutional *Missachtung* that operates below the level of explicit denial."
2. **Skizze #2 (~M27, mid-Jaeggi):** Forms of life and problem-solving — e.g., "Jaeggi's problem-solving criterion has a hidden requirement of *legibility*."
3. **Skizze #3 (~M30, post-Jaeggi):** The Honneth-Jaeggi disagreement — pre-work for the M37–38 Comparison Exercise.
4. **Skizze #4 (~M31, alongside Module 9):** Ideology critique or lay normativity, drawing on Celikates.
5. **Skizze #5 (~M32) [BONUS — opt-in]:** Social-science methodology drawing on Taylor — pre-work for W3.2 topic candidates. The M35 R5 retreat's topic-firm-up exercise covers this; opt in only if M32 capacity allows.

**Phase III Skizzen merge into W3.2 work.** From M35 onward, further Skizze attempts subsume into W3.2 drafting (each major section begins as a stand-alone Skizze). Cadence becomes topic-driven.

**Hallucination caveat.** When asking Claude to flag whether your positive claim has been made elsewhere, treat as a starting hypothesis to verify, not ground truth.

**Filing:** `drafts/skizze-XX.md`. The best Skizzen (surviving pressure-testing) are raw material for W3.2 spines.
