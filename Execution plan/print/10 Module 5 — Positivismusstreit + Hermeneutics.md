# Module 5 — Positivismusstreit + Hermeneutics

## Module 5 — Positivismusstreit + Hermeneutics (W50–W58)

**Why this module matters (sequencing).** The methodological hinge of the whole programme, placed last in Phase I so that the Motivational Checkpoint (W59–61) can test it immediately against your own practice: the *Positivismusstreit* and the Habermas–Gadamer debate define the epistemological space (against positivism *and* against purely hermeneutic self-understanding), and the *EuI* Nachwort is the pivot into Module 6's TKH. Protect under pressure: the four *EuI* Fulls; the Gadamer Orientation has an explicit minimum-cut rule (W52). [Full rationale → syllabus](sozialphilosophie_syllabus.md#module-5-the-positivismusstreit-the-hermeneutics-debate-and-habermass-epistemological-turn).

### Capability targets — by end of Module 5 you should be able to:

- [ ] State Habermas's three knowledge-constitutive interests (technical, practical, emancipatory) and the corresponding forms of science.
- [ ] Explain why Adorno thinks value-freedom in social science is impossible (not merely undesirable), and where the substantive disagreement with Popper lies (deeper than "facts vs. values").
- [ ] State Gadamer's *Wirkungsgeschichte* and explain why Habermas thinks hermeneutic understanding must be supplemented by explanatory social theory (the move that motivates ideology critique).
- [ ] State what the Nachwort to *Erkenntnis und Interesse* retracts from the 1968 argument, and why the retraction is the move into the Habermas of TKH.
