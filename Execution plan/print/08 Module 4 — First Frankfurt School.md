# Module 4 — First Frankfurt School

## Module 4 — First Frankfurt School (W37–W44)

**Why this module matters (sequencing).** The direct predecessor of everything Phase II reads: Marx + Weber + Lukács + psychoanalysis combined to explain why emancipation failed, and the distinctive method (immanent critique, ideology critique, pathology diagnosis) stated as a programme. It sits here because 3A.1 is its immediate premise, and because the W40 *ND* decision and the Freyenhagen `→ M37–38` tags feed the Comparison Exercise. Protect under pressure: the 4.1 Horkheimer Full (the second "what is critical theory?" foundational note) and the DdA Lights; the Benjamin pair and MM degrade first. [Full rationale → syllabus](sozialphilosophie_syllabus.md#module-4-the-first-frankfurt-school--critical-theory-as-programme).

### Capability targets — by end of Module 4 you should be able to:

- [ ] State what makes theory "critical" in Horkheimer's 1937 sense, and distinguish this from Popperian critical rationalism (where "critical" means open to falsification).
- [ ] Explain the thesis that "Enlightenment reverts to myth" — as a *structural* argument, not a slogan — and identify what the structural claim commits its proponents to.
- [ ] Distinguish Adorno's negativism (filtered through Freyenhagen) from Habermas's later constructive turn, and articulate what each gains and loses.
- [ ] Locate Benjamin's "Theses on History" within the developmental-narrative debate that Allen later reactivates against Habermas and Jaeggi.
