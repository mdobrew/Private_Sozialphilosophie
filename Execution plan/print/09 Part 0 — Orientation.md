### Capability targets — by end of Part 0 you should be able to:

- [ ] State Honneth's distinction between Sozialphilosophie, political philosophy, moral philosophy, and empirical sociology, and explain what work the concept of "social pathology" does that no neighbouring concept can.
- [ ] State Jaeggi's three modes of critique (functional, moral, ethical) and identify which mode a given critical claim is operating in.
- [ ] Locate the early Frankfurt School institutionally (Jay) and explain how its institutional structure related to its intellectual programme.

### Why this module matters
Part 0 is orientation, not foundation — its job is to give you a *map* of where Sozialphilosophie sits (against political philosophy, moral philosophy, empirical sociology) and a first encounter with the field's central diagnostic category (social pathology) before you spend a year reconstructing the canonical texts. Honneth's "Pathologien" is the first text that gets a Full-tier reconstruction; the reconstruction you write at W5 is the central year-end calibration object (re-read at R3 and end of Phase III).

> [!tip] Phase IV — connections flagged in this module
> Jay ch. 3 (Institute's pre-WWII engagement with *Bildung* and culture) → H.6.1 Adorno "Theorie der Halbbildung" (late-1950s sequel).
