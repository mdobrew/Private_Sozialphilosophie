### Why this module matters
Honneth's fundamental claim is that Habermas located normativity at too abstract a level (the presuppositions of discourse). The more basic normative structure of social life is **recognition** (*Anerkennung*): humans develop intact self-relations (self-confidence, self-respect, self-esteem) only through being recognised by others in three spheres — love, rights, solidarity. Social pathologies are forms of systematic *Missachtung*. This provides the normative criterion for critical social philosophy without requiring the demanding rationalist apparatus of discourse ethics — and it's the framework you'll most likely position your own contribution against (or alongside) in W3.2.
