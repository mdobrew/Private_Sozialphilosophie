### Comparison Exercise design

**Goal.** Build the positioning skill needed for original work. Harder than reconstruction (W3.1), easier than original critique (W3.2). The skill: *holding two compatible-seeming frameworks far enough apart that you can locate the genuine point of disagreement, then judging whether the disagreement is resolvable, and if so on whose terms.*

**Phase A — Materials assembly (~3h).**
Pull together: (a) Module 7 Full-tier reconstruction notes on recognition spheres + normative reconstruction; (b) Module 7 reconstruction on Honneth *RdF* methodological introduction; (c) Module 8 Full-tier notes on Jaeggi problem-solving model + Allen's critique; (d) Zurn 8.3 reconstruction; (e) Laitinen & Särkelä 8.4 reconstruction; (f) Method note from W1 (M35–37).

**Phase B — Reading-pass on flagged passages (~4–6h).**
Re-read targeted passages (already tagged `→ M37–38` during Phase II):
- Honneth *KuA* chs. 5–6 (Missachtung → struggle for recognition as the engine of moral development).
- Honneth *RdF* methodological introduction (normative reconstruction).
- Jaeggi *KvL* ch. 3 ("forms of life can fail") + chs. 8–10 (problem-solving model).
- Zurn (second-order disorders).
- Allen *End of Progress* ch. 6 (the critique of the problem-solving model's implicit progress story).

**Phase C — Comparison essay (~6–8h, ~3,000 words).**
Structure:
1. **Reconstruct each thinker's account of social pathology** in 600 words each. Be precise about: (i) what makes something pathological; (ii) what's required for it to be diagnosed; (iii) what the theorist's normative apparatus needs to do the work.
2. **Identify the agreements.** Both: pathology is institutional, not just personal; both reject pure functionalism; both reject pure moralism; both are committed to immanence (the standard for critique is internal to social practices).
3. **Identify the disagreements at three levels:**
   - *Conceptual:* recognition vs. problem-processing as the diagnostic primitive. What does each register that the other misses?
   - *Methodological:* normative reconstruction vs. quasi-pragmatist immanent critique. Where do they read the *same* practice differently?
   - *Philosophy-of-history:* does either require a developmental story? Allen's challenge applies asymmetrically.
4. **Take a position.** Where is the disagreement resolvable, and on whose terms? Where is it irreducible? What does the irreducibility tell you about the field's open questions?
5. **Locate yourself.** Where does the ECB / monetary-policy / financial-stability material from your Field Report bite differently on the two frameworks?

**Phase D — Filing (~1h).**
File the essay. **This essay is the proof-of-positioning that you need before W3.2.** It is also the first draft of part of W3.2's introduction.
