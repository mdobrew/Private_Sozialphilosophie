# Part 0 — Orientation

## Part 0 — Orientation (M1–M2, W1–W7)

**Why this part matters (sequencing).** Part 0 inverts the front-load-the-canon default: a map of the field and a first encounter with its central diagnostic category (social pathology) *before* a year of reconstructing the canon, so every Part I text arrives with a motivating question attached. It also boots the calibration infrastructure the W13 gate depends on (Reading Log from day one; the W5 Recon supplies the overhead metric and is the central year-end calibration object, re-read at R3 and end-Phase III). Protect under pressure: the 0.2 Full Recon and the log discipline; the Jay chapters are the first trim. [Full rationale → syllabus](sozialphilosophie_syllabus.md#part-0-orientation-weeks-14-34-hours).

### Capability targets — by end of Part 0 you should be able to (test at R1):

- [ ] State Honneth's distinction between Sozialphilosophie, political philosophy, moral philosophy, and empirical sociology, and explain what work the concept of "social pathology" does that no neighbouring concept can.
- [ ] State Jaeggi's three modes of critique (functional, moral, ethical) and identify which mode a given critical claim is operating in.
- [ ] Locate the early Frankfurt School institutionally (Jay) and explain how its institutional structure related to its intellectual programme.
