### Why this module matters
Module 9 (Social Epistemology) addresses the question of who has epistemic access to social pathology, and whether subjects' self-understandings can be trusted or are ideologically distorted. Module 10 (Philosophy of Social Science) is the methodological hinge that relates normative-diagnostic claims to causal-explanatory claims — *Taylor's "Interpretation and the Sciences of Man" is the single most provocative text in Phase II for an economist* and pre-loads the W3.2 phenomenon-work directly. Streeck and Polanyi provide the historical-institutional foundations for capitalism-critique that Phase III's Module 14 / Appendix D engages.
