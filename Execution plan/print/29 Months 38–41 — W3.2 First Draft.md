**Framing.** This is the **first draft of a piece you intend to present and publish.** Target: 7,000–10,000 words. Target audience: scholars working on capitalism critique / social pathology debates. Target venues for eventual submission: *WestEnd*, *Constellations*, *ZkSP*, *Philosophy & Social Criticism*.

**M38 (~5h):** Topic lock-in and scoping. Choose the W3.2 topic from your candidates. Possible angles (calibrated against your Field Report and ECB knowledge):
- Forward guidance / central bank communication as a case of (Habermasian) communicative practice subject to (Habermasian) colonisation by systemic imperatives.
- ECB price-stability mandate as normatively reconstructed market governance (Honneth-style) — and where Jütten's critique bites.
- "Financial stability" as a quasi-pathology category: what's lost when a normative concept becomes a technical management target?
- Central bank independence as institutional Sittlichkeit (Hegelian-Honnethian) under conditions of legitimacy strain.
- DSGE / structural-modelling conventions as a form of life with problem-processing failures (Jaeggi-style) — connecting to Taylor's "Interpretation" critique.

**M39 (mid-month) — Conference logistics deadline:** Survey CFPs for Tagungen / workshops in months M44–M48. Submit abstracts to **1–2** venues. Candidate venues:
- FAPTF (Frankfurter Arbeitskreis für politische Theorie und Philosophie).
- DGPhil Sektion Sozialphilosophie workshop.
- IfS or ConTrust workshop.
- MPIfG interdisciplinary workshop (Beckert's institute — natural fit for an economist working on critique).
- *Critical Horizons* / *Constellations* workshop (less common but worth checking).

**M39–40 — Drafting at weekly pace:** ~10h/week of focused drafting, building out section by section. Skeleton:
1. Introduction: the practice, why it matters, what the piece will do (~1,000–1,500 words).
2. Reconstruction of the practice's self-understanding (~1,500 words).
3. Immanent critique: the tension on the practice's own terms (~2,000 words).
4. Structural explanation: why systematic, not accidental (~2,000–2,500 words).
5. Stakes: what the diagnosis implies for the practice and for the field (~1,000–1,500 words).
6. Conclusion: open questions / what the critique cannot yet do (~500 words).

**M40 — Writing Block W1 (1 week of leave, ~30h concentrated drafting).** This is the *coherence-producing* block — daily 1-hour fragments cannot generate a 7,000-word philosophical argument with a single argumentative arc. Use the week to consolidate the skeleton into a complete first draft. 5 days × 6 hours.

> [!important] Leave-booking lead time for W1
> **W1 falls in ~September 2029.** Pre-book leave by **June 2029** (3-month lead time). If M39 CFP submission slips by >2 weeks, re-book W1 to the next leave-eligible non-GovC week (likely October–November 2029).

**M41:** Polish first draft. Identify 2–3 trusted readers (a philosophically inclined colleague at the ECB if available; a contact from the Vorlesung / Oberseminar; a member of FAPTF). Send draft for informal-presentation prep.

**Phase IV → Appendix C item C.6 Brenner / Appendix C item C.10 Heller / H.6.8 Stojanov:** depending on W3.2 topic, flag specific Phase IV readings that would deepen the piece. Track in a "Phase IV reading horizon" doc.
