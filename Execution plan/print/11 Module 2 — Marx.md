### Why this module matters
Marx is not merely a historical predecessor but an active reference point for Jaeggi, Fraser, and the ongoing debate about whether and how capitalism can be criticised from within. Jaeggi's *Entfremdung* is explicitly a rehabilitation of the Marxian concept. The critique of political economy — reading economic categories (commodity, value, money) as expressions of social relations — is the template for the Frankfurt tradition's approach to social analysis, and the move you will recognise in nearly every later thinker.

> [!note] Economist's note
> "Entfremdete Arbeit" is short but compressed. Marx's four dimensions of alienation (from product, from activity, from species-being, from other humans) are *not* a typology of bad working conditions — they describe what *labour as such* becomes under capitalist conditions. Jaeggi later strips out the species-being assumption while retaining the diagnostic.

> [!note] Economist's note
> Resist treating Marx's "value-form" as a primitive theory of money supply. The argument is logical, not historical: *if* social production is organised through private exchange, *then* a general equivalent must emerge, and *then* social relations between persons appear as relations between things. The empirical history is a different question (one Heinrich, next week, will help separate).
