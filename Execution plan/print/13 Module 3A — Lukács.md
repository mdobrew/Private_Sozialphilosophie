### Why this module matters
Lukács's *Geschichte und Klassenbewußtsein* (1923) is the single most important mediating text between Hegel/Marx/Weber and the Frankfurt School. Lukács transforms Marx's analysis of commodity fetishism and Weber's diagnosis of rationalisation into a *total* diagnosis of bourgeois consciousness: the reification (*Verdinglichung*) of social relations under capitalism is not merely an economic phenomenon but pervades the entire structure of modern thought. This is the argument Horkheimer and Adorno radicalise in *Dialektik der Aufklärung*. Without Lukács, the transition from Marx and Weber to the first Frankfurt School has a missing step, and the concept of *Verdinglichung* — as it recurs in Honneth (2005), Berger & Luckmann, and Jaeggi's critique of Honneth — lacks its proper genealogy.
