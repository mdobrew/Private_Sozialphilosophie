# Spines, Paradigms & Endpoint Branches — a field map

**What this is.** The navigational layer of the programme: a map of the intellectual traditions ("spines") in and around Frankfurt-tradition Sozialphilosophie, which one this programme is built on, which are rivals, and what each would cost to enter. Open it to *orient* — to remember where you are and what your options are — not to look up a text (that is the syllabus) or a schedule (that is the execution plan). Created 2026-06-29.

**Role in the document architecture.** Sits beside [sozialphilosophie_syllabus.md](sozialphilosophie_syllabus.md) (the bibliography), [importance_tiers.md](importance_tiers.md) (Phase-III importance + the endpoint-branch map in §4), and [execution_plan.md](execution_plan.md) (the schedule). This doc is the "where am I in the field and what are my choices" layer the others presuppose but do not state.

**How it connects.**
- The *texts* for any spine live in the syllabus; this map indexes them by **lineage** (§3) rather than by module.
- The *branch-points* (which spine you specialize toward in Phase III) are operationalized in **importance_tiers.md §4** (conditional promotions → endpoint-branch map).
- *Debates* you actually work out become **Practice C debate-maps** in the Obsidian vault (`Debatten-Karte/`); §6 here is only a watchlist of what is live.

## 1. What a "spine" is — spine vs. track vs. coverage

- **Spine** — the one lineage you become *fluent* in and write *from*: a method + its home problems + a voice. You get one; reaching the frontier in 3.5 years on two is not feasible.
- **Track** — a conditional thematic commitment (a problem-area you point your spine's lens *at*: capitalism, social reproduction, democratic legitimacy…). You might commit to one or two. The phase3 §4 "conditional promotions" are the track-markers.
- **Coverage** — awareness-level engagement with *rival paradigms* (Modules 10–12, 11A–D): enough to position against them and satisfy referees, not fluency.

**The lens/field distinction.** Spine ≈ the lens you think *with*; track ≈ the field you point it *at*. Caveat: the "hard" tracks (Marxism, genealogy, Adornian negativism) carry their *own* lens, so committing to them deeply turns a track into a rival spine — which is why Marxist/CPE is best read as a *latent second spine*, not just a track.

**This programme's spine:** **recognition / normative reconstruction** — Hegel → Marx → Lukács → first Frankfurt School → Habermas → Honneth → Jaeggi → Forst; method = immanent critique / normative reconstruction; central grammar = *Anerkennung* (recognition) / *Kritik von Lebensformen* (critique of forms of life). Monocentric by design.

## 2. The spine inventory

Six spines with a real claim on a Frankfurt contribution. **Equip-level** = how far the current syllabus takes you. **Wedge** = where *this reader* (a quantitative macroeconomist) has an unusual opening.

### A. Recognition / normative reconstruction — *your spine*
Method: read the rational potential immanent in existing practice, criticize its betrayal. Equip-level: **fluent** (the whole 1–8 spine). The endpoint default.

### B. Capitalism-as-form / critique of political economy  — ★ *current keeper*
Method: critique of social *form* — value-form analysis, real abstraction, abstract/impersonal domination (not violated recognition-claims). Equip-level: **strong, near co-primary** (Module 2 + 13 + 14 + Appendix C). To reach frontier-fluent: the value-form / Frankfurt-Marxism wing (Sohn-Rethel, Arthur, Bonefeld, *Nach Marx*) + the economist-wedge pair (Engster, Lapavitsas). **Wedge:** real abstraction as an *operation* you can specify (aggregation, index theory, structural-vs-reduced-form); the **central bank as the missing institution of value-form theory**; "money as measure" (Engster) interrogated with real measurement theory. Venue: *Historical Materialism*.

### C. Power / genealogy  *(Nietzsche → Foucault → Saar)*
Method: genealogy + analytics of power; suspicion of normative foundations; expose contingency. Equip-level: **aware** (11A Foucault + Allen as the bridge + Nietzsche 4.14). To fluency: Foucault's *Naissance de la biopolitique*, Saar (*Genealogie als Kritik* 2007; *Was ist Sozialphilosophie?* 2025), Honneth *Kritik der Macht* (1985), Koopman *Genealogy as Critique* (2013). **Wedge:** genealogy/biopolitics of *economic governance* — central-bank independence as a depoliticization device; inflation-targeting / the NAIRU as a *veridiction regime*; forward guidance as conduct-on-conduct. Venue: *Foucault Studies*.

### D. Adornian negativism
Method: determinate negation — know the wrong without a positive theory of the good (*Bilderverbot*). The *principled opposite* of reconstruction. Equip-level: **foil only** (DdA, *Minima Moralia*, *Negative Dialektik* selections, Freyenhagen; now Freud 4.13 + Nietzsche 4.14). To fluency: full *Negative Dialektik*, the moral-philosophy lectures (*Probleme der Moralphilosophie*), the live Gordon↔Freyenhagen exchange. **Wedge:** *Identitätsdenken* / the *Tauschprinzip* ≈ quantification — "quantification as identity-thinking" (what is lost when the qualitatively non-identical is made commensurable for a model). Venue: *Critical Horizons*, *Adorno Studies*.

### E. Socialisation / Bildung / social reproduction  — ★ *current keeper*
Method: formation-of-persons — how social conditions form (or deform) the subjects the normative order presupposes; *Bildung* as emancipatory formation against adaptive *Halbbildung*. Equip-level: **Phase-IV appendix → building out** (Appendix H, now load-bearing). Through-line: psychoanalytic micro-foundation → social-reproduction (material site) → Bildung (normative category) → developmental-empirical (reality-check). **A thin but high-opportunity space** — Bildung is nearly absent from current Frankfurt work. **Wedge (the flagship):** a recognition-theoretic critique of the *human-capital* paradigm of childhood, using the toxic-stress / ACE evidence as the immanent pressure point — Heckman's early-childhood-investment programme has empirically rediscovered that early recognition relationships are decisive for formation, while theorizing it in a vocabulary (investment, returns, skill-stock) that negates the *Bildung* it stumbled onto. Triangulation — Frankfurt Bildung × recognition theory × the actual economics of human-capital formation — with no current occupant. Keystone primary: Heydorn, *Über den Widerspruch von Bildung und Herrschaft* (1970).

### F. Sub-tracks
Decolonial (8A), deliberative-democracy / legitimacy, social suffering (Renault), work-and-recognition (*Der arbeitende Souverän*) — narrower; hosted *by* a spine rather than standing alone.

**Are these exclusive or exhaustive? Neither.** The best figures bridge (Allen = recognition + genealogy + Adorno; Fraser = recognition + Marxist; Forst = Habermasian + recognition). And "spine" is *purpose-relative* — others one could name: pragmatist social philosophy (Dewey → Mead → Särkelä; Jaeggi's problem-solving is already pragmatist), feminist/care + social-reproduction, republican/neo-Roman (Pettit, Skinner), critical philosophy of race / Afropessimism, Spinozist/affect (Saar's *Immanenz der Macht*, Lordon), critical phenomenology (Guenther). The six above have the strongest claim on *this* endpoint.

**Rival paradigms ≠ spines.** Modules 10–12 / 11A–D (interpretive philosophy of social science; systems theory; critical sociology; critical realism; analytic political philosophy) are *coverage*, not candidate spines. Adopting one as your *primary lens* generally moves you off social-philosophy-proper — toward sociology (Luhmann, who is *anti-*normative on principle), philosophy of social science (Bhaskar), or analytic political philosophy (Rawls/Pettit, a different normative field Frankfurt defines itself against). What keeps a project *social-philosophical* is the **normative-diagnostic vocation** (diagnosing pathology / orientation to emancipation). Using a rival paradigm as a *tool within your spine* is legitimate and encouraged (the bridge document's Woodward / mechanism moves); making it your *lens* is exit.

## 5. Methods — where they oppose, where they combine

- **Hard opposition:** reconstruction (A) vs. negativism (D). You pick a side. Live frontier: Gordon↔Freyenhagen.
- **Bridgeable tension:** reconstruction (A) vs. genealogy (C) — "where does genealogy get its own critical normativity?"; welded by Saar / Allen / Koopman / Owen.
- **Family quarrel:** reconstruction (A) vs. discourse-proceduralism (Habermas) — Forst is the standing synthesis (Habermasian justification + Kantian floor + genealogy of *who* gets to justify).
- **Pattern:** practitioners are method-pluralist within a home spine; the tradition advances by hybridizing.
- **Hazard (your idiom):** do not read these methods through decision theory — Habermas's procedure is reason-giving, not incentive-compatible; genealogy's "could-have-been-otherwise" is not a clean intervention claim; real abstraction is not representative-agent aggregation. These are the [Economist's note] traps.

## 6. Live frontier-debate register (watchlist — not worked maps)

- **Adorno's positivity:** Gordon, *A Precarious Happiness* (2023) vs. Freyenhagen, "Misinterpreting Negativism" (*EJP* 2024) — is there a positive moment in Adorno's negativism, or not? (Spine D; bears on A.)
- **Genealogy's normativity:** Saar / Allen / Koopman / Owen — can genealogical contingency-exposure ground critique without a foundation? (Spine C vs. A.)
- **Recognition vs. impersonal domination:** can recognition theory reach value-form / abstract domination? Fraser, Postone, *Nach Marx* (2013). (Spine A vs. B.)
- **Democracy without shortcuts:** Lafont vs. epistemic democracy (Landemore / Estlund) — bears on technocratic economic governance. (Habermasian.)

→ Work any of these as a Practice C debate-map (`Debatten-Karte/`) when you reach it; this register only tracks what is open.

## 7. The shared core and the branch points

Modules 1–8 + the Phase-I instrumental readings = the **shared spine-core** for branches A, C, D, E and largely B. You commit a branch at R5 (~M35), execute it as the Phase III contribution, and deepen it in Phase IV. The branch-by-branch item promotions live in **importance_tiers.md §4** (endpoint-branch map + conditional promotions).

**Current keepers: B (capitalism-as-form) and E (Bildung).** Their frontier readings are folded into the syllabus (Module 13/14 for B; Appendix H for E); the other branches stay map-only until chosen.
